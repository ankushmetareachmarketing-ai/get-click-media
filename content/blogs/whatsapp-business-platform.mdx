## WhatsApp Business Platform vs WhatsApp Business App — The Full Picture

Meta offers three WhatsApp products for businesses. Understanding which one applies to your business is the first step.

**WhatsApp (Consumer)** — for individuals, not for business. Personal messaging only, with no business features: no catalog, no verified name, no API. It cannot be used for business communication at scale and violates Meta's terms if used for bulk messaging. Messaging limit: N/A, personal use only. API access: none.

**WhatsApp Business App** — free download for small businesses. A free app for Android and iOS with a business profile (address, hours, description), a catalog for up to 500 products, quick replies and away messages (basic auto-reply only), and broadcast to up to 256 saved contacts. It runs on a single device with one user at a time, has no API access, no CRM integration, and no advanced automation. Suitable for businesses handling under 50 WhatsApp conversations per day. Messaging limit: 256 broadcast contacts maximum. API access: none — manual management only.

**WhatsApp Business Platform** (formerly WhatsApp API) — for medium to large businesses, accessed via a BSP. Full programmatic API access via REST API and webhooks, unlimited contacts and message volume on a tiered scale, verified business identity with Green Tick option, a multi-agent shared inbox with unlimited simultaneous agents, full chatbot automation with AI and rule-based flows, [CRM integration](/whatsapp-crm-integration) (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, custom), [WhatsApp Catalog and Commerce](/whatsapp-catalog) with unlimited products, WhatsApp Flows for interactive in-chat forms, [Click-to-WhatsApp Ads](/whatsapp-click-to-whatsapp-ads) integration, and real-time analytics. Requires a Meta-authorised BSP — Get Click Media handles onboarding. Messaging limit: 1,000 per day (Tier 1) scaling to unlimited. API access: full REST API + webhooks + Catalog API + Flows API.

## WhatsApp Business Platform — Key Components

The WhatsApp Business Platform comprises several interconnected components. Understanding each helps you plan your implementation.

1. **WhatsApp Cloud API.** Meta's cloud-hosted API infrastructure — the primary way most businesses access the Platform today. Messages route through Meta's servers, it's always on the latest version, and there's no infrastructure maintenance or setup cost. Get Click Media uses Cloud API exclusively for all client onboarding; On-Premise API was deprecated by Meta for new accounts.
2. **WhatsApp Business Account (WABA).** The business entity registered on Meta's platform. Your WABA holds your business name, phone number, brand assets, and template library. You own your WABA — it's registered under your Meta Business Manager. Get Click Media is listed as the BSP on your WABA but doesn't own it. If you switch BSPs, your WABA — with all its templates and history — transfers with you.
3. **Phone number and sender identity.** Your Platform account is tied to a dedicated phone number, not shared with other businesses, displaying your verified business name on every message. You can use a mobile number, landline, or VoIP number. The number cannot be simultaneously active on WhatsApp Business App or consumer WhatsApp.
4. **Message templates.** Business-initiated messages must use Meta-approved templates — pre-approved message formats with variable placeholders for personalisation. Categories: Marketing (campaigns), Utility (transactional alerts), and Authentication (OTPs). Get Click Media manages template submission and approval, with most templates approved in 1–3 business days.
5. **WhatsApp Flows.** An interactive in-chat form builder enabling multi-screen forms inside WhatsApp for address capture, appointment booking, lead qualification, feedback collection, and checkout — no external URL required. Flows integrate natively with the Platform API and deliver structured JSON data to your backend. Available to all WhatsApp Business Platform accounts.
6. **WhatsApp Commerce and Catalog.** A product catalog and in-chat shopping cart built into the Platform. Connect to your product database via Catalog API for automatic sync — customers browse, add to cart, and send orders, all within WhatsApp, with payment via a payment link (Razorpay, PayU, UPI) or WhatsApp Pay. See the full [WhatsApp Catalog](/whatsapp-catalog) and [WhatsApp Commerce](/whatsapp-commerce) breakdowns.
7. **Business Manager integration.** Your Platform account lives inside Meta Business Manager alongside your Facebook and Instagram assets. This enables [Click-to-WhatsApp Ads](/whatsapp-click-to-whatsapp-ads) — Facebook or Instagram ads that open WhatsApp conversations — shared audience targeting, and unified Meta billing for all marketing spend.
8. **Webhooks and real-time events.** Every message event — sent, delivered, read, button tap, reply, opt-out — is pushed to your webhook endpoint in real time as a JSON payload. Your CRM, analytics system, or custom application receives these events and acts on them immediately, enabling true real-time conversation intelligence.

## Frequently Asked Questions — WhatsApp Business Platform India

**What is the difference between WhatsApp Business Platform and WhatsApp Business API?**
They are the same product under different names. Meta rebranded WhatsApp Business API to WhatsApp Business Platform in 2023 to reflect the expanded capabilities beyond raw API access — including WhatsApp Flows, WhatsApp Commerce, and AI-powered tools. All documentation, pricing, and technical specifications remain the same.

**Who can use the WhatsApp Business Platform?**
Any legitimate business with a Meta-verified business identity can access the Platform through an authorised BSP. There is no minimum company size or revenue requirement. The practical requirement is a registered business entity, a business website, and a GST certificate or company registration document for Meta verification.

**Can I use the WhatsApp Business Platform if I already have WhatsApp Business App?**
Yes — they are separate products and can coexist, but not on the same phone number. Your WhatsApp Business App number cannot be simultaneously active on the Platform; it must first be deregistered from the App. Most businesses use a separate dedicated number for the Platform to keep manual and automated communication cleanly separated.

**Does WhatsApp Business Platform work across all industries in India?**
Yes, with minor restrictions. Most industries can use all Platform features. Regulated industries — banking, insurance, pharmaceuticals, medical devices — have additional template compliance requirements and cannot promote certain products without regulatory disclaimers. Adult content, gambling, and illegal products are prohibited across all industries.

**What is Meta Business Manager and do I need it?**
Meta Business Manager, now called Meta Business Suite, is Meta's central platform for managing your Facebook Page, Instagram account, Ad account, WhatsApp Business Account, and Commerce catalog. Yes, you need it — the Platform requires a verified Meta Business Manager account. Get Click Media guides you through setup, or links your new WABA to an existing account.

**What is the difference between the WhatsApp Cloud API and On-Premise API?**
Cloud API is hosted on Meta's servers — no infrastructure management, always on the latest version, lower latency. On-Premise API required businesses to host the API themselves, with more control but significant operational overhead. Meta deprecated On-Premise API for new accounts in 2023. Get Click Media exclusively uses Cloud API.

**How does WhatsApp Business Platform pricing work?**
Pricing is conversation-based — you pay per 24-hour conversation window, not per message. There are four categories: marketing (₹0.79–0.91 per conversation), utility and authentication (₹0.14–0.17 per conversation), and service conversations, free for the first 1,000 per month. Get Click Media charges a platform fee in addition to Meta's conversation rates. Full breakdown on our [pricing page](/whatsapp-api-pricing-india).

**Can I switch from one BSP to Get Click Media without losing my account?**
Yes. WABA migration between BSPs is supported by Meta. The process transfers your WABA — with all templates, message history, and phone number registration — from your current BSP to Get Click Media, typically in 1–3 business days, with no changes needed on your development side and no downtime.

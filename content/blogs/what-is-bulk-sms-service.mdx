Bulk SMS service has become the backbone of business communication in India. From retail chains sending offer alerts to banks delivering OTPs, every industry relies on SMS to reach customers instantly. In this guide, we break down exactly what bulk SMS service is, how it works, and why it remains India's most cost-effective communication channel.

## What is Bulk SMS Service?

Bulk SMS service is a platform that enables businesses to send thousands- or even millions- of text messages simultaneously to a targeted list of mobile numbers. Unlike individual SMS sent from a phone, bulk SMS uses an SMS gateway connected directly to telecom networks, ensuring high delivery speed and reliability.

**Key characteristics of bulk SMS service:**

- **High-volume delivery**- send from 100 to 10 million+ messages in one campaign
- **Instant reach**- messages are delivered within seconds of dispatch
- **No internet required**- recipients receive SMS on any mobile phone, including feature phones
- **98% open rate**- SMS messages are read within 3 minutes on average
- **Pan-India coverage**- reach all telecom operators: Airtel, Jio, Vi, and BSNL

## Types of Bulk SMS in India

### Promotional SMS

Promotional SMS is used for marketing campaigns- discount offers, product launches, event invites, and brand awareness. Under TRAI regulations, promotional SMS can only be sent between 9 AM and 9 PM and only to users who have opted in to receive marketing messages.

### Transactional SMS

Transactional SMS is used for critical communications- OTPs, bank alerts, order confirmations, appointment reminders, and delivery updates. These can be sent 24/7, including to numbers on the DND (Do Not Disturb) registry.

### Service Implicit and Service Explicit SMS

Introduced under TRAI's DLT framework:

- **Service Implicit**- messages where prior consent is implicit (e.g., OTPs, account alerts)
- **Service Explicit**- messages requiring explicit user consent (e.g., service updates, newsletters)

## How Bulk SMS Marketing Works

Bulk SMS marketing follows a simple but powerful process:

1. **Build your contact list**- collect opt-in mobile numbers from customers
2. **Register on DLT**- complete TRAI's mandatory registration
3. **Create and approve your template**- pre-register all message content
4. **Schedule your campaign**- use an SMS panel or API to send at the right time
5. **Track results**- monitor delivery reports, click-through rates, and conversions

## DLT Compliance: What You Need to Know

Since 2021, TRAI mandates that all commercial SMS senders in India must register on the Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) platform. This involves:

1. **Entity Registration**- register your company on a telecom operator's DLT portal
2. **Sender ID Approval**- get your 6-character alphanumeric sender ID approved
3. **Template Registration**- pre-register all message templates before sending

Without DLT compliance, your messages will be blocked by the telecom network. A good bulk SMS service provider handles DLT setup for you as part of onboarding.
